Abstract

This paper studies models of the efficient allocation of resources following the imposition of a tax. We consider several market arrangements, in each case quantifying the loss of efficiency associated with the imposition of the tax. We find as markets become coupled, the cross price elasticities and the relationships between firms are significant for the placement of the tax. We illustrate our results with a numerical example.
