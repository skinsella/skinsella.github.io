Yesterday students alerted me to the fact that some key readings weren't available in the library. I asked our librarian, Peter Reilly, about this, and here's the important bit from his mail:

> I was aware that the four copies of Macroeconomy of Eurozone were
> missing, but we cannot remove the individual record from the catalogue
> system
> I replaced these four missing items last week.
> There are now twelve copies of the book placed in Short Loan (1 day).
> Two of these are currently borrowed so at the moment there are ten
> residing on the shelf and trolley.
> I think the problem could be that the students glanced at the catalogue
> saw four were missing and did not go to the end of the record.

I went to the library myself this morning, and books by Mankiw, *Macroeconomics*, Blanchard, *Macroeconomics*, and Abel and Bernanke, *Macroeconomics*, as well as by Krugman and Wells, *Macroeconomics*, are available, and these are all pretty close substitutes.
