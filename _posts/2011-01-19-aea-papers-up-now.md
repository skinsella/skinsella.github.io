The American Economic Association meetings are where the mainstream of the economics profession gets together to push the discipline forward, eat cheese, and get hammered away from their wives, husbands, and partners. [The papers are all available here](http://www.aeaweb.org/aea/2011conference/program/meetingpapers.php). Two I've read so far are Robert Shiller's [Economists as Worldly Philosophers](http://www.aeaweb.org/aea/2011conference/program/retrieve.php?pdfid=490) and Anthony Atkinson's [The Restoration of Welfare Economics](http://www.aeaweb.org/aea/2011conference/program/retrieve.php?pdfid=231). Both are superb and worth reading through.
