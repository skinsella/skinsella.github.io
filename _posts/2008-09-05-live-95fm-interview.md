Here's an interview I did this morning on [Live 95FM with Ed Myers](http://www1.live95fm.ie/index.asp), following on from[a previous post](http://www.stephenkinsella.net/2008/09/03/budget-move-not-good-news-at-all/) about the move of the budget.

My main point was the government needs to strike the correct balance of decreased [public spending](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_spending), increased taxes, wage [moderation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moderation), and [borrowing](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Debt). [Ireland](http://maps.google.com/maps?ll=53.0,-7.0&spn=10.0,10.0&q=Ireland&t=h) has a pretty low [national debt](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_debt), so borrowing is the soft option. However, it doesn't address the medium term problems of a high wage, high price economy.
