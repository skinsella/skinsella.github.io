Some thoughts on using Wolfram's typesetting software, [Publicon](http://www.wolfram.com/products/publicon/index.html).

Publicon is a pimped version of [Mathematica](http://www.wolfram.com/products/mathematica/index.html)'s front end, a graphical interface for the processing wizard that is the Mathematica kernel. I've tried it now for a while, and my thoughts on it are pretty negative, which I didn't want. I was looking for a way to write some of my papers without having to enter the cumbersome latex markups which generate the mathematical notation you see in some of my papers. I didn't find it here. Publicon has a lot of cool features, like different stylesheets for different article styles, and 'pallettes' of different notations and structural elements, which is why I downloaded the thing in the first place.

The problem is the export features: Publicon just doesn't play well with professional-grade 

![](https://www.stephenkinsella.net/wp-content/plugins/latex/cache/tex_c51d7e23458ca0e7373a8ed6ab56b2b9.gif)

, so, for me, it's not a runner.

Sad.

Some issues with the software:

1. Too expensive

2. Bibliography integration sucks

3. Latex export sucks.

4. Basic features of typesetting missing.

So, Publicon is still no way near persuading me to go from 40 dollar [textmate](http://www.google.ie/url?sa=t&ct=res&cd=1&url=http%3A%2F%2Fmacromates.com%2F&ei=IXpvSIvwK5HWQJn0zN8C&usg=AFQjCNEORMq1DJ65XRHEXU2uoKZNIePB_A&sig2=oTIJ9xAw3K-rPF-iXa3hfg)+free latex solution.

The search continues.
