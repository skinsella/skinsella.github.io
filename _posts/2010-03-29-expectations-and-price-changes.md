Financial Economics students, we've constantly been stressing the role of expectations about the future in pricing assets in financial markets. [Tomorrow](http://www.stephenkinsella.net/2010/03/28/super-tuesday/) it is widely expected that [AIB](http://www.google.com/finance?q=NYSE:AIB) will have a large chunk of its ownership transferred into the hands of the Irish government. Consequently, the prices of Irish banking stocks are [going south](http://r.zemanta.com/?u=http%3A//abcnews.go.com/Business/wireStory%3Fid%3D10229215&a=15621574&rid=95651771-32f3-42fd-82ce-3510fd27b054&e=546631ccdc06d8739ae0b46781dac020).

[Click here to look at a live updating chart of AIB's share price against that of Bank of Ireland.](http://finance.yahoo.com/q/bc?s=AIB&t=5d&l=on&z=m&q=b&c=BOI)
