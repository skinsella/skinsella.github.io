A quick update[on this post](http://www.stephenkinsella.net/2008/08/27/1229/). I'd like to give an example of what I mean by a rebate program to help stimulate the housing market.

*Example.* Adam and Barbara are a thirty something couple with a combined income of 48,000 euros per year. They have two children, and want to buy a house for their growing family. This family will pay approximately 14,000 euros in [income tax](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Income_tax) this year.

If we allow them to offset the cost of mortgaging a new [property](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Property_tax) against these (already paid) taxes, the government effectively increases Adam and Barbara's [incomes](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Income) by, say, 10,000, and only in the direction the government wants: funneled through the housing market. The [tax rebate](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tax_refund) does not apply for other purchases and at other times.
