The [19th Hebrew University summer school in economics](http://www.as.huji.ac.il/schools/econ19/) has videos of all of its presenters, amongst whom are 6 [Nobel Laureates](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Nobel_laureates) in economics. The summer school is organised around the economics of [Kenneth Arrow](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenneth_Arrow), right, and well worth watching. My favourite video so far is [Herbert Scarf](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herbert_Scarf)'s presentation on [The Core](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computable_general_equilibrium).

[Link to the videos](http://www.as.huji.ac.il/schools/econ19/media.php), via [Gill Kalai](http://gilkalai.wordpress.com/2008/07/15/arrows-economics-1/)

Also, Scarf's master work, and an index to a large part of the recent [history of economic thought](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4XhvG_fD0HA), is [here](http://cowles.econ.yale.edu/P/cm/cfmmain.htm).

Even cooler, [here's a game you can play](http://cs-people.bu.edu/paithan/spernerGame/SpernerGame.html)to learn [Sperner's Lemma](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sperner's_lemma).
