Further reading on the water shortage issue [I posted about earlier on](http://www.stephenkinsella.net/2008/09/03/yet-more-scaremongering-futureshock-the-last-drop/)convinces me that thinking about water shortages is not advanced enough to be credible.

This story, hoisted from a comment at [Watering Down](http://www.climate-resistance.org/2008/08/watering-down-development.html), is instructive:

> Sydney’s huge [Warragamba dam](http://maps.google.com/maps?ll=-33.8834,150.596&spn=1.0,1.0&q=Warragamba%20Dam&t=h) was completed in early 1960 when the population was about one million. We have had rapic[sic] [population growth](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Population_growth) and now, with no new storage facilities being built, we suffer occasional water shortages.
> These water shortages are not caused by the population quadrupling. No way. They are really caused by fossil fuel induced [AGW](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Actual_gold_weight) causing drought. No politician in [Australia](http://maps.google.com/maps?ll=-35.3,149.133333333&spn=10.0,10.0&q=Australia&t=h) would seriously propose building anything as radical as a new dam. Instead, we are building a huge sea water filtration which might provide 10% of Sydney’s fresh water needs - if required. The recent torrential rain has reduced the urgency of the plant but population is still growing and it might get dry again at some time in the future.
> The filtration plant will be powered by electricity from our [coal burning power plants](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fossil_fuel_power_plant).
> You couldn’t make this stuff up if you tried.

No you couldn't.
