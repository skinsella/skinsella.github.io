The [Common Agricultural Policy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_Agricultural_Policy) is a source of much contention in [Europe](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europe) and in the world because of its size, budgetary implications, and redistributive effects. It was a key feature of the recent [Doha Development Round](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doha_Development_Round) of trade talks. Read more about the CAP by downloading [this .pdf from the EU](http://ec.europa.eu/agriculture/publi/capexplained/cap_en.pdf). Here is[an excellent Q & A session](http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/4407792.stm) by the BBC about the CAP. 

[![Common Agricultural Policy](http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/CAPspendingbysector.png/202px-CAPspendingbysector.png)](http://commons.wikipedia.org/wiki/Image:CAPspendingbysector.png)

Image via Wikipedia

In this lecture, we talked about the history of the CAP, its current status, and looked at a measure of just how much individuals are getting from the CAP in places like Limerick and Dublin using [farmsubsidy.org.](http://www.farmsubsidy.org) Most of you felt that individual farmers shouldn't have their names made public. I disagreed, but 5 of you changed your minds on the issue, so that's something 🙂
