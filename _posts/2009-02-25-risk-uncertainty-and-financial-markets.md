Here's Prof. Peter Klein, showing us that there are people and personalities behind the current collapse. Some of them, of course, are economists and mathematical finance geeks.

> A quick follow-up to [Nicolai’s post on the copula function](http://organizationsandmarkets.com/2009/02/24/copula-functions-and-the-current-crisis/): See also this item on [Gary Gorton’s role in the financial crisis](http://organizationsandmarkets.com/2008/11/03/beware-of-geeks-bearing-formulas/), which includes Warren Buffett’s great line: “Beware of geeks . . . bearing formulas.” And items on Knightian uncertainty [here](http://organizationsandmarkets.com/2007/08/22/knightian-financial-markets/) and [here](http://organizationsandmarkets.com/2008/03/19/numbers-dont-lie-or-do-they/).
> And there’s this passage from Darren Aronofsky’s cult classic [Pi](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0138704/):
> 
> Restate my assumptions: One, Mathematics is the language of nature. Two, Everything around us can be represented and understood through numbers. Three: If you graph the numbers of any system, patterns emerge. Therefore, there are patterns everywhere in nature. Evidence: The cycling of disease epidemics;the wax and wane of caribou populations; sun spot cycles; the rise and fall of the Nile. So, what about the stock market? The universe of numbers that represents the global economy. Millions of hands at work, billions of minds. A vast network, screaming with life. An organism. A natural organism. My hypothesis: Within the stock market, there is a pattern as well. . . . Right in front of me . . . hiding behind the numbers. Always has been.
> 
> This is before the speaker, the mathematician protagonist Max Cohen, goes literally insane. That’s what quantitative financial modeling can do to you. http://feeds.wordpress.com/1.0/goreddit/organizationsandmarkets.wordpress.com/4678/
