Prof. James Hamilton digests the pure rage AIG's decision to pay its executives 165 million dollars in bonuses, some of them to people who no longer worked for AIG. [Hamilton writes](http://www.econbrowser.com/archives/2009/03/aig_outrage.html):

"One of the reasons this is so outrageous is that the promise of such bonuses was in fact one of the [very factors that caused our current problems](http://www.econbrowser.com/archives/2009/01/executive_compe.html), creating incentives for managers of AIG to get out of solid insurance underwriting and into [hedge fund gambling](http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2009/03/03/AR2009030303810.html). If anyone had supposed that AIG had "learned its lesson", this report seemed to dash that hope against the wall like a plate of china."
