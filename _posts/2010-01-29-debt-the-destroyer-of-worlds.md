EC6012 students should take a look at this article on debt and trade deficits, on how the US, with a very low [savings rate](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saving), and already up to its eyeballs in debt, can experience an* improvement* in its [trade deficit](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balance_of_trade) over 2009, mainly by [printing money](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantitative_easing). Put these two charts from [Mike's post](http://mikeashton.wordpress.com/2010/01/25/i-am-become-debt-destroyer-of-worlds/) side by side, and you'll see why. The US is printing M1-money, which is going into Treasuries, and storing up debt, which may lead to a US-specific meltdown in currency terms in the next 3 or 4 years. This is a worry for everyone, because the old phrase about the US sneezing and everyone else getting a cold is true, especially for Ireland. We'll most likely get Man-Flu though. Unless, of course, deficits don't matter for the US.
