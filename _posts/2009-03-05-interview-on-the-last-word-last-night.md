With [Alan Ruddoc](http://www.independent.ie/topics/Alan+Ruddock)k of the [Sunday Independent](http://unison.ie/sunday_independent), I made the following points on last night's show

1. First, the government has to be careful who far it digs down into the[income distribution](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Income_inequality_metrics)looking for increased[taxation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tax)revenue, because the lower one drills down, the more likely you are to create poverty traps, where it is less profitable to work than to stay on benefits.
2. Second, the government is sure to increase[VAT](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Value_added_tax)and[Excise duties](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Excise)on cigarettes, petrol, and other consumables. Combined with a rising[unemployment rate](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unemployment), and a decrease in[disposable income](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disposable/Discretionary_income)as a result of increased taxe rate and widened tax bands, domestic demand will get hammered on 3 fronts, which will cause yet more unemployment as businesses see demand for their products and services slide further.

As [Michael Taft](http://notesonthefront.typepad.com/politicaleconomy/2009/03/there-is-an-almighty-locomotive-train-of-a-consensus-coming-down-the-tracks-at-us-taxation-many-commentators-are-demandin.html) correctly observes, no government ever deflated its way out of a [recession](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recession). The changes we will have to make are on the public expenditure side, but also, I would now argue, on the monetary side. [Ireland](http://maps.google.com/maps?ll=53.0,-7.0&spn=10.0,10.0&q=53.0,-7.0%20%28Ireland%29&t=h) is a member of the [European Union](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union).
