Reading [Peter Albin](http://www2.nysun.com/article/71753) and Duncan Foley's [Barriers and Bounds to Rationality](http://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/redirect.html%3FASIN=0691026769%26tag=adriaantijsse-20%26lcode=xm2%26cID=2025%26ccmID=165953%26location=/o/ASIN/0691026769%253FSubscriptionId=0PZ7TM66EXQCXFVTMTR2) made me want to do research in complex adaptive systems. It also formed my decision to go to the [New School for Social Research](http://newschool.edu/gf/econ) in New York for graduate school.

You can see the effects of Albin's work in the [Society for Computational Economics,](http://comp-econ.org/) and the [Santa Fe](http://www.santafe.edu) school. It should be rememebered that Albin was doing research into [Cellular Automata](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cellular_automata) (via the game of life) a decade before the complexity revolution took place, although is was predated by Thomas Schelling.

To get a sense of the depth of Albin's research program, read [this](http://homepage.newschool.edu/~foleyd/GECO6335/albfolintro.pdf), by Duncan Foley.

There will be an academic memorial to Prof. Albin on May, 20, 2008, at 4pm in the John Jay College of Criminal Justice, 899 10th Avenue, room 630.

A beautiful paper, showing Albin (and Foley) at their best, is
