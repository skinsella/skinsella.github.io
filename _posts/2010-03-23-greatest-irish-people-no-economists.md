I'm shocked-shocked!-that no economists made [the greatest Irish people](http://www.rte.ie/tv/irelandsgreatest/)*[evar](http://www.rte.ie/tv/irelandsgreatest/)* list. But they decided to leave out [Hamilton](http://go2.wordpress.com/?id=725X1342&site=litmuspaper.wordpress.com&url=http%3A%2F%2Fen.wikipedia.org%2Fwiki%2FWilliam_Rowan_Hamilton) and [Walton](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ernest_Walton), and put [Louis Walsh](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louis_Walsh) and Daniel O'Donnell in, so I think it's fair to say they weren't really thinking hard when drawing up the list.[Mary Mulvihill has more details](http://marymulvihill.net/2010/03/22/no-scientists-on-rtes-greatest-irish-shortlist/). All apart from Dr Whittaker are currently deceased, excepting any zombie-related incidents.

But if--if!--there was an economist in the room, this is part of the list he/she/it would surely have  come with. Or something.

1. [Roy Geary](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roy_C._Geary), for his contributions to statistics and setting up the ESRI. Update. Read more on Dr Geary[here](http://www.economics.ox.ac.uk/members/peter.neary/papers/pdf/geary97.pdf).
2. [Francis Edgeworth](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francis_Ysidro_Edgeworth), for his development of Mathematical Psychics, later bastardised into Walrasian[general equilibrium theory](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_equilibrium_theory). Every student of economics should read Edgeworth's rant about pleasure chariots before asking just how the pleasure chariots got turned into[indifference curves](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indifference_curve).[The story is here](http://books.google.com.tw/books?id=GkrYxL0QtpcC&printsec=frontcover&dq=Machine+Dreams&cd=1#v=onepage&q=&f=false), if you're interested, but it's a long book.
3. [WM Gorman](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/W._M._Gorman), the developer of the[Gorman conditions](http://cepa.newschool.edu/het/profiles/gorman.htm)for utility functions and preference fields.
4. [John Elliot Cairnes](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Elliott_Cairnes), often talked about as the last of the classical economists, which isn't quite right, Cairnes' best book by far was[The Slave Power](http://books.google.com.tw/books?id=YWck2O52tOAC&printsec=frontcover&dq=Cairnes+the+slave+power&source=bl&ots=8_O2c26H11&sig=FymYFHhfSzQid8vApTEdIqkwvcY&hl=zh-TW&ei=PLSoS5DdG8-LkAX4xeWcAw&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=1&ved=0CAYQ6AEwAA).You can read it yourself to find out why.
5. [Richard Cantillon](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Cantillon). Developed the first fully worked out[theory of long run equilibrium](http://homepage.newschool.edu/het/essays/youth/cantsys.htm)(ie when capital, land, and labour are fully mutable)
6. [TK Whittaker](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T._K._Whitaker). Saved the Irish State. Full stop. Update. Dr Whittaker is still kicking, all apologies, cheers[Dr Lurve](http://www.ucd.ie/research/people/economics/drkevinjmdenny/).
7. [Sir William Petty](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Petty)who, though a subject of the Crown, lived and worked most of his life in Ireland. Cheers to[Ronan Lyons](http://ronanlyons.com)for the suggestion. Petty was the first real macroeconomist and public finance theorist.

Louis Walsh put Boyzone *and* Westlife  *and* Samantha Mumba together. I mean, they had good(ish) tunes, but they're not really in the same league, no?

Anyone I missed?
