EC6012 Lecture 2 *Review of Intermediate Macroeconomic Concepts*

**Introduction**

Macroeconomics is the study of fluctuations in the economy as a whole. The key to understanding fluctuations is the accounting matrix. This lecture is about connecting the macroeconomic concepts you would have seen in an intermediate economics class using a textbook like[Leddin and Walsh](http://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/redirect.html%3FASIN=071713492X%26tag=ws%26lcode=xm2%26cID=2025%26ccmID=165953%26location=/o/ASIN/071713492X%253FSubscriptionId=02ZH6J1W0649DTNS6002) or ["Macroeconomics (4th Edition)" (Olivier Blanchard)](http://www.amazon.com/gp/redirect.html%3FASIN=0131860267%26tag=ws%26lcode=xm2%26cID=2025%26ccmID=165953%26location=/o/ASIN/0131860267%253FSubscriptionId=02ZH6J1W0649DTNS6002), and the approach of Godley and Lavoie.

We'll use the example of the multiplier to make the connections crystal clear. Before that, we'll take a brief tour through James Tobin's Nobel lecture, highlighting passages which guides and gives a methodological definition to the structuralist approach.

**Aim**

To bridge the orthodox macroeconomic treatment of the multiplier, with the structuralist treatment employed by Godley and Lavoie.
