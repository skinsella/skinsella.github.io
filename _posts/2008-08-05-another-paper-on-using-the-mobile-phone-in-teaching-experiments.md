[![Seperated From The Pack](http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3047/2735209707_46cf5a307d_m.jpg)](http://www.flickr.com/photos/85976136@N00/2735209707/)Dr. [Stephen Cheung](http://www.econ.usyd.edu.au/staff/scheung/) has a fascinating paper on the use of a mobile phone messaging system in his classes, which readers of this blog [will know I'm interested in](http://www.stephenkinsella.net/2008/07/03/many-to-one-using-the-mobile-phone-to-interact-with-large-classes/).

Here's the abstract and a link to the paper:

**Abstract:** The author demonstrates how mobile phone text messaging can be used to overcome the limitations of pencil-and-paper experiments without incurring the costs of full computerization. Students submit responses as text messages, which are downloaded into a spreadsheet for automated analysis and receive feedback by return messaging.

Citation: Stephen L. Cheung, Using Mobile Phone Messaging as a Response Medium in Classroom Experiments, *Journal of Economic Education* Volume: 39, No. 1, Issue: Winter 2008, Pages: 51-67. (.[pdf)](http://stephenkinsella.net/WordPress/wp-content/uploads/2008/08/jee-on-mobiles.pdf)
