Just ahead of next week's EC4004 jump into inequality and [macroeconomics](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macroeconomics), here's a great talk on the processes of actually feeding a city (ht: [Tim Berry](http://timberry.bplans.com/2009/10/on-cities-food-history-and-future.html)). Important bits from the talk:

1. We lose about 47 million[acres](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acre)of[rainforest](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rainforest)every year. And at the same time, we lose about 50 million acres of farm land to[salinization](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil_salinity)and[erosion](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erosion).
2. Half the[food](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Food_industry)produced in the[USA](http://maps.google.com/maps?ll=38.8833333333,-77.0166666667&spn=10.0,10.0&q=38.8833333333,-77.0166666667 (United%20States)&t=h)is thrown away.
3. A billion of us are[obese](http://www.wikinvest.com/concept/Obesity)while another billion starve.
4. 80% of[global trade](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_trade)in food is controlled by five corporations.
