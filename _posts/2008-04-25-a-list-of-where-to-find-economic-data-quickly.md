---

1. The US Economy:[Bureau of Labor Statistics](http://www.bls.gov/)
2. The EU:[Eurostat](http://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/)
3. The EU with Micro data:[OECD](http://www.sourceoecd.org/database/OECDStat).
4. World Poverty/Income Distribution data:[IMF](http://www.imf.org/external/ns/cs.aspx?id=28)
5. World GDP/GNP Data:[The Penn World Tables](http://pwt.econ.upenn.edu/)
6. Historical Financial Data:[Measuring Worth](http://www.measuringworth.com/exchangeglobal/?q=hmit/exchangerates)
7. Irish Macro Data:[CSO](http://www.cso.ie/)(Measuring Ireland's Progress is a great place to start here), the[ESRI](http://www.ESRI.ie)has excellent Micro data on health.
8. Current Financial Data: I use[Yahoo finance](http://finance.yahoo.com/)or[Financial Data Finder](http://fisher.osu.edu/cgi-bin/DB_Search/db_search.cgi?setup_file=finance.setup.cgi). Both are excellent.
9. Different Interest Rate Data:[FRED](http://research.stlouisfed.org/fred2/categories/22).
10. Internet Price Competition Index:[Nash-Equilibrium](http://www.nash-equilibrium.com/).

I'll add more as I come across them.
