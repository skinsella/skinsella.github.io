Here's a guest lecture for the 4rth year module Ireland and the World Economy, and it is be all about fiscal policy: what it is, where Ireland stands right now, and what we might do about it in the next few years. We'll talk about the connection between sovereign debt and banking bailouts, and discuss alternative fiscal rules. [Here are the lecture notes.](http://stephenkinsella.net/WordPress/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/IWE_Lecture_WK3_Kinsella.pdf)

The readings for the lecture are:

P Lane and A Benetrix, International differences in fiscal policy during the global crisis. [[pdf](http://stephenkinsella.net/WordPress/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/iiisdp336.pdf)]

P Lane, A new fiscal strategy for Ireland, [[pdf](http://www.esr.ie/Vol40_2/Vol%2040-2-Lane.pdf)]
