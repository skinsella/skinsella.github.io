In today's [Irish Times,](http://www.irishtimes.com/newspaper/opinion/2009/0127/1232923366898.html)[Fintan O'Toole](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fintan_O%27Toole) calls for a bold restructuring of the [Irish economy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_the_Republic_of_Ireland) to cope with what will almost certainly be it's worst performance in [GDP](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gross_domestic_product) terms for 60 years.

Fintan proposes a basic effective [income tax](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Income_tax) of 25% on every one, with high earners having their incomes capped at 80,000 for two years. Scrapping the tax shelters, inefficient tax breaks, and whacky [subsidies](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subsidy) like  the Horse and Greyhound fund (70 million euros? Really?) should go a long way towards plugging the 20 billion euro gap in the public finances. Finally, scrap wasteful overlaps of public and private life, and introduce universal [preschool education](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Preschool_education) where now only a child benefit allowance  exists.

The plan is bold, it is thoughtful, and it might change some minds.

Does it change yours?
