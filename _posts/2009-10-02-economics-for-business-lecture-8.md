In this lecture we looked at the [Market for Lemons](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Market_for_Lemons), based on [Akerlof](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Akerlof)'s 1970 paper, which you can get [here](http://www.econ.ox.ac.uk/members/christopher.bowdler/akerlof.pdf). The basic role of information in market failure and in particular with regard to uncertainty of quality made a bit of an impact. I heard some awful chat up lines, and we made a strategy for date-quality success.
