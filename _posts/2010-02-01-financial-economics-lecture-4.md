This lecture is all about introducing you to something you won't find anywhere else--the stock market from a physicists viewpoint. Some notes are [here](http://www.stephenkinsella.net/2008/08/01/limit-order-only-markets/), the big set of notes are [here](http://stephenkinsella.net/WordPress/wp-content/uploads/2010/02/EC4024_2010_Lecture4.pdf). This lecture is based on MFM Osborne's pioneering work in econophysics in the 1970's, [his best work is here](http://www.amazon.com/gp/redirect.html%3FASIN=0964629208%26tag=adriaantijsse-20%26lcode=xm2%26cID=2025%26ccmID=165953%26location=/o/ASIN/0964629208%253FSubscriptionId=0PZ7TM66EXQCXFVTMTR2). [Here](http://stephenkinsella.net/WordPress/wp-content/uploads/2010/02/EC4024_2010_Lecture4_Pres.pdf) are the lecture slides.

If you'd like to know more about econophysics, check out the introductory pieces [here](http://www.unifr.ch/econophysics/paper/list). [This paper](http://arxiv.org/abs/0709.3662) is harder, but well worth digging into if you like thinking this way.
