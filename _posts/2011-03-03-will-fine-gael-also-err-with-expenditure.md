By the end of this week, [Ireland](http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/ireland) will have a new government, with a new mandate, new ministers, and a new purpose.

 The parties making up the incoming government have made promises to the electorate. Some were clearly fanciful, others less so.

 This blog looks at which ones we should care to remember, which promises should be forgotten, and which ones should be thrown back in their faces in 12 months' time? What should we watch for?

 Let's look at three things really matter: the new government's policy on the EU/IMF loan package, public sector reform, and its decision on whether and where to cut government expenditure.
