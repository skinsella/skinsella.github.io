(with [Matthias Greiff](http://matthiasgreiff.wordpress.com/) and [EJ Nell](http://www.newschool.edu/NSSR/faculty.aspx?id=16350&DeptFilter=NSSR+Economics))

Abstract. We model a [macroeconomy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macroeconomics) with stock flow consistent [national accounts](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_accounts) built from the local interactions of heterogenous agents (households, firms, bankers, and a [government](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government)) through product, labor, bond, and money markets in [discrete time](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discrete_time). We use this model to show that, without any restrictions on the type of interactions agents can make, and with [asymmetric information](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_asymmetry) on the part of firms and households in this [economy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy), [power-law](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_law) dynamics with respect to firm size and firm age, [income distribution](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Income_distribution), skill set choice, returns to innovation, and [earnings](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Income) can emerge from multiplicative processes originating in the [labor market](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Labour_economics).

**Keywords:** Inequality, Agent Based Macroeconomics, Econo-Physics.
