This lecture builds on last week's introduction to international [monetary economics](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monetary_economics) by introducing a basic macroeconomic framework based on [national accounts](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_accounts), then moves on to contrast two ways of thinking about macroeconomic relationships--post-Keynesian and Neoclassical--and then finishes with the main workhorse model of monetary economics, [IS-LM](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IS/LM_model), which we extend slightly. [Here](http://stephenkinsella.net/WordPress/wp-content/uploads/2010/02/international_ecs_guest_lecture.pdf) are the lecture notes, more information on Bernanke-Blinder is [here](http://stephenkinsella.net/WordPress/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/islm-beyond.pdf), and [Here](http://stephenkinsella.net/WordPress/wp-content/uploads/2010/02/EC6012_2010_Lecture2.pdf) are the slides.
