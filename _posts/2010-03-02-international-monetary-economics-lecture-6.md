Exchange rate determination is extremely important in the theory of monetary economics, and we'll look at some theories of exchange rates tomorrow. Get the slides [here](http://stephenkinsella.net/WordPress/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/EC6012_2010_Lecture6.pdf), a mathematica notebook for the lecture is here, and the data I used on exchange rates [are here](http://www.oanda.com/currency/historical-rates). Play about with it to get a sense of what's happening. [Here's the Wolfram|Alpha code, too](http://www.wolframalpha.com/input/?i=Dollar%2FEuro). Messing about with these online toys is a great way to understand CIP and UIP better. Finally, [Stockman's (1978) theory of exchange rate determination is here](http://www.oanda.com/currency/historical-rates). The important reading for this lecture is Mishkin, chapter 19[.
