The 'two souls' equation I showed in class today relates the real wage divided by average labour productivity to the return on productive capital divided by the rate of capacity utilisation. Adding these two ratios together gives you 1. I've been sent an email to explain my use of the 'two souls' epithet. It comes from [Goethe's](http://hermetic.com/sabazius/goethe.htm) *Faust*:

Two souls, alas, are housed within my breast, 
And each will wrestle for the mastery there. 

So there you have it.
