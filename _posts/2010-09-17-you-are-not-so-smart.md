Well worth a read through when considering economic modeling. Much of what we do as economists is to find various bullet holes and draw targets around them, using statistics. Much of the time this is a useless activity, but sometimes, we do very well indeed.

> The Misconception: You take randomness into account when determining cause and effect.
> The Truth: You tend to ignore random chance when the results seem meaningful or when you want a random event to have a meaningful cause.

via [The Texas Sharpshooter Fallacy « You Are Not So Smart](http://youarenotsosmart.com/2010/09/11/the-texas-sharpshooter-fallacy/#more-689).
